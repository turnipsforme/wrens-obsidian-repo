# non stick pan

- **Always cook with oil.** We recommend using 1½ teaspoons to 1 tablespoon of high-heat oil (such as grapeseed, avocado, canola, or algae) every time you cook. Through regular cooking, microscopic layers of oil will bond to the surface of your pan to eventually create a slick, naturally nonstick surface.
- **Always preheat your pan over medium or medium-low heat before adding oil.** This will take about 1-3 minutes depending on your stove. When you hover your hand directly above the surface of the pan, it should feel hot.
- **Use metal or other sturdy tools.** Because this pan has no coatings, you can safely use metal tools—in fact, a thin metal utensil releases food significantly better than plastic or silicone tools, which often just push food around.  
- **Condition your pan early and often.** “Conditioning” is the term we use to describe the process of quickly heating your clean, dry pan on the stovetop, then rubbing in a small amount of high-heat oil with a paper towel Conditioning your pan after each cook early on will noticeably boost your pan’s nonstick properties.

That’s your pan’s natural seasoning building up. With regular cooking over time, the entire surface of your pan will develop a dark patina—and become increasingly nonstick.

- **If food sticks, don’t despair.** Some foods (like eggs, chicken breast, tofu, or potatoes) are more prone to sticking than others. We highly recommend conditioning your pan after each cook early on. We’ve found this significantly boosts the pans nonstick properties and reduces sticking. The more you cook, the more nonstick your pan will become over time.
- **Avoid acidic foods at first.** We recommend holding off on cooking with acidic foods like lemons, tomatoes, and wine early on while your pan is still developing its seasoning. Avoid boiling water, sauces, or other liquids for long periods, as this can strip the pan’s seasoning. If your pan ever looks a little dry or streaky, don’t worry—simply condition it a few times to restore its slick surface.
